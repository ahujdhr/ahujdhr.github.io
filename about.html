Who I Am

My name is Dhruv Ahuja and I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I will continue on to a master's degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering. Born in the Midwest and now studying in Atlanta, I’ve always been captivated by the interplay between hardware and software. From dismantling old gadgets to building my first line‑following robot, curiosity has driven me toward engineering since childhood.

This ePortfolio reflects my journey through academia and research. I am particularly interested in robotics, computer vision, optimization, and control because they combine mathematical rigor with hands‑on experimentation. My projects range from active mapping algorithms that enable robots to explore unknown spaces to dynamic gap planning for autonomous vehicles. I’m committed to using technology to improve people's lives and to staying curious about emerging ideas.

Beyond the lab, I enjoy exploring new technologies, playing tennis, and volunteering at STEM outreach events. These activities keep me balanced and remind me that innovation is rooted in human experiences. I value collaboration and believe that diverse teams produce the most creative solutions.

As you explore this site you'll find examples of my coursework, research, and professional experiences. I hope this glimpse into my work sparks new connections. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to discuss potential collaborations or share ideas about robotics and autonomous systems.
