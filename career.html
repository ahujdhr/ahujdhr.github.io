My Vision

My long‑term goal is to become a leading engineer and innovator in the field of robotics and autonomous systems. I aspire to develop technologies that improve efficiency, safety, and quality of life. Ultimately, I plan to pursue graduate studies and contribute to cutting‑edge research or lead engineering teams in industry.

Short‑Term Goals & Timeline

To achieve this vision, I've outlined a series of short‑term goals and milestones:

  • 2025–2026: Complete my B.S. in Electrical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science, continue undergraduate research in active mapping and autonomous driving, and maintain a strong GPA.
  • 2026–2027: Pursue a master’s degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering focusing on advanced robotics and machine learning; take graduate coursework and develop leadership skills through teaching or mentorship.
  • 2027–2030: Continue on to a Ph.D. program or join a research‑oriented role in industry; apply and expand knowledge in computer vision, control systems, and optimization; publish research and present at conferences.
  • 2030 and beyond: Lead autonomous systems projects in academia or industry, mentor junior engineers and students, and contribute to ethical and impactful deployment of robotics technologies.

In addition to technical milestones, I aim to develop leadership and entrepreneurship skills. I plan to join organizations such as IEEE Robotics and Automation Society and participate in hackathons and startup incubators. By networking with professionals, attending workshops, and staying current with emerging trends, I will prepare myself for a dynamic career path.

I believe that success requires continuous learning, curiosity, and adaptability. This ePortfolio will track my progress over time and serve as a reminder of the steps I'm taking toward my goals. I'm excited about the journey ahead and eager to face the challenges that will shape me into a skilled and compassionate engineer.
