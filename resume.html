Research & Academic Projects

My research spans active mapping, autonomous driving, and robot navigation. In the Robot Learning and Reasoning Lab I helped build an active mapping pipeline that determines where a robot should move next to maximise information gain. This work leverages 3D Gaussian Splatting and entropy‑based scoring functions to select both the next best view and the next best trajectory.

At the Intelligent Vision and Automation Laboratory I collaborated with industrial partners to develop planning algorithms for self‑driving cars. Our dynamic gap planner processes perception data from cameras and LiDAR to find safe windows in traffic and generates smooth trajectories using differential flatness and feedback control.

Earlier in my studies I worked in the Laboratory for Intelligent Decision and Autonomous Robots, where we integrated model‑predictive control into a gap‑based planner for human‑aware navigation. These experiences have given me a broad foundation in perception, optimization and control.
